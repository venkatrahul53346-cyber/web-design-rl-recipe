Where Opus wins, where it loses — per-task score distribution

What to look at

  • 11 tasks, sorted by mean. Green = saturated/easy, red = hard.
  • Every task lives between the two dotted lines: screenshot-embed cheat = 0.05 on the left, oracle = 1.000 on the right.
  • Saturated tasks (auth (glassy), pricing (dark)) sit near the top with tight spreads (~0.05–0.09).
  • Hard tasks have wider spreads — docs (mono), portfolio (neobrut), government (dark) all show 0.20+ best-vs-worst.
  • The single 0.000 outlier on saas (minimal): an Opus run that produced no HTML at all. The grader correctly returned its true score.
  • Takeaway: A reward signal that saturates on 3 control tasks and discriminates on 8 hard ones is exactly the shape an RL trainer wants.

What within-task spread is made of — best vs worst on 3 high-spread tasks

What to look at

  • 3 rows = 3 tasks with the largest best-vs-worst gap.
  • Each row: best Opus run (left), worst Opus run (right), per-signal contribution to the spread.
  • In the bar chart, raw delta (faded) and weighted contribution (solid) per signal.
  • Look at bm_position in every row — it's the longest bar by far. Opus produces the right elements but places them slightly off.
  • bm_recall is second — the worst run matches less of the GT's element area than the best.
  • bm_text and bm_border barely move — Opus reproduces copy and rounded-corner aesthetics near-perfectly on every run.
  • Takeaway: The gap between a good Opus run and a poor one is almost entirely about spatial composition, not copy or styling.

The grader is correctly anchored — calibration on auth_glassy

What to look at

  • Same task, five different agent submissions, sorted by score.
  • Gibberish (random ASCII, no CSS): 0.032. Every signal hits zero.
  • Screenshot embed (<img src=GT.png>): 0.050. The strongest cheat possible — pixel-perfect visual_ssim of 1.0. But text=0, recall=0, tree_bleu=0. The 0.05 weight on SSIM × 1.0 is the entire score.
  • Structure only (GT layout, text shuffled): 0.678. Layout signals fire correctly; text and SSIM are penalised.
  • Real Opus 4.7 mean over 10 attempts: 0.819.
  • Oracle (GT verbatim): 1.000.
  • Takeaway: Floor and ceiling are correctly placed. The single most powerful trivial cheat scores 1/20 of the real run. The grader cannot be gamed without actually doing the task.

What Opus 4.7 consistently struggles with — canonical failure modes

What to look at

  • Four canonical failure modes, each shown via the worst Opus run on a task that exhibits it.
  • Symmetric drift — when GT is intentionally asymmetric (portfolio neobrutalism), Opus collapses to a 12-col grid.
  • Column compression — when GT uses narrow editorial measure (~40ch news columns), Opus defaults to wide single-column.
  • Hero compression — when GT has tall airy hero (government civic page), Opus crams content upward into the fold.
  • Density underproduction — when GT shows 3-pane mono (docs), Opus collapses to dark single column.
  • All four are caught by the same two signals: bm_position and bm_recall.
  • Takeaway: Spatial composition is the consistent gap. The failure modes connect cleanly to the signals doing the discriminative work in slide B.

Where the score actually comes from — per-signal discrimination

What to look at

  • Two views of the same 106 trials.
  • Left — share of composite variance attributable to each signal. Three signals do 85% of the work: bm_position (54.7%), bm_recall (20%), tree_bleu (10.5%).
  • Right — Spearman ρ between each signal and overall composite. bm_text at +0.90 correlates strongly, but its low standard deviation means it doesn't separate runs — it's a quality indicator, not a discriminator.
  • bm_border shows ρ = −0.17 — slightly negative correlation. This is why v5.1 dropped its weight to 0.
  • Takeaway: The weights are calibrated to put discriminative work where the variance lives, with low-variance high-correlation signals as quality floors.

v5.2 candidate — area-weighted per-pair aggregation

What changes (and what doesn't)

  • Matching step is unchanged. Same Hungarian + Sørensen-Dice on text bigrams, same 0.5 threshold, same anchors.
  • Aggregation across matched pairs changes from np.mean to np.average(weights = sqrt(GT bbox area)) for the six per-pair signals: bm_position, bm_text, bm_color, bm_font, bm_border, bm_size.
  • Page-level signals untouched: bm_recall was already area-weighted; tree_bleu and visual_ssim aren't per-pair.
  • Why sqrt-area, not pure area: a hero with 100× the bbox area of a footer link gets ~10× weight under sqrt, not 100×. Avoids a single huge element dominating every signal.
  • Why GT area, not agent or min/max: we're measuring fidelity to GT. Agent-side area is already represented inside bm_size.
  • Implementation: ~10 lines in grade_page. The w and h fields per anchor are already captured by _RICH_JS — we just weren't using them outside bm_recall.
  • Motivating concern: at flat-mean aggregation, an H1 mismatch counts the same as a footer-link mismatch. A reader notices the H1 first.

v5.1 vs v5.2 empirical comparison — 39 trials regraded with both modes

What to look at

  • Top-left scatter: v5.1 vs v5.2 composite. Spearman ρ = 0.9984, Kendall τ = 0.9838. Every point on y=x.
  • Top-middle: deltas are tiny — mean +0.003, range [−0.004, +0.017]. v5.2 nudges scores slightly upward on average.
  • Top-right: within-task ranking integrity. Best stayed best on all 4 tasks. Worst stayed worst on 3 of 4. The auth_glassy swap is between two trials whose v5.1 scores differed by 0.002 — essentially tied.
  • Bottom-left, the meat: per-signal deltas show where the change actually happens. bm_color +0.015 and bm_position +0.014 lead — both rise under area-weighting because Opus does better on bigger anchors than smaller ones.
  • bm_text (−0.011) and bm_size (−0.014) drop slightly — bigger anchors have noisier text Dice and bigger absolute size mismatches.
  • bm_recall, tree_bleu, visual_ssim show zero delta — they aren't per-pair signals.
  • Verdict: v5.2 is a safe upgrade. Ranking preserved, no pathological cases, signal effects are interpretable. Worth shipping, but v5.1 isn't broken.

Summary — grader properties demonstrated

  • Higher score ↔ more faithful replica. Pair-by-pair visual inspection across 11 tasks confirms the score ranking matches the human ranking.
  • Floor and ceiling correctly anchored. Trivial cheats fall below 0.10; oracle hits 1.000; the SSIM-cheat scores exactly the SSIM weight ceiling.
  • Discriminative within tasks. Hard tasks show 0.10–0.23 best-vs-worst spread, dominated by spatial-composition signals.
  • Identifies model failure modes. The same two signals (bm_position, bm_recall) that drive discrimination also catch all four canonical Opus failures.
  • Anti-gaming by construction. No single signal can produce a high composite — every cheat we tried collapses to ≤0.05.
  • Cross-model rank-ordering. haiku < sonnet < opus on every task individually (REPORT.md §4.4b).
