QUESTIONS

Before you
hit submit.

The five things we get asked most often before teams join the beta. If something here doesn't cover it, write to hello@pliny.dev — a real person will reply.

How long until I actually get access?+
Median onboarding is 2.5 days. We're approving in batches of ten because every team gets a 30-minute call with an engineer who tunes Pliny against your monorepo's quirks. If you need to skip the line for a procurement deadline, mention it in the bottleneck field and we'll do what we can.
Does Pliny train on my code?+
No. Your repository content is used only to build a per-tenant context index that lives in your dedicated namespace. It is never used to train base models, never shared across customers, and is deleted within 72h of account closure. The full data flow is in our security memo.
Can I bring my own model keys?+
Yes, on Team and Enterprise. We support Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT-4-class, and any OpenAI-compatible endpoint (vLLM, Ollama, Together, Bedrock). Bring your keys, we don't mark up tokens, you see exact spend per repo in the dashboard.
What does it cost to be in the beta?+
Beta seats are $24/seat/mo with no minimum and no commitment — you can cancel any month. Beta pricing is locked for the life of your account, including after we hit GA in Q1 2026 and list prices move up.
We're on self-hosted GitLab. Will this work?+
Yes. Pliny ships as a self-hosted helm chart for teams who can't send code outside their VPC. We support GitLab CE/EE, GitHub Enterprise Server, and Bitbucket Data Center. Plan on a one-hour install with someone from our team on the call.
Who's actually behind this?+
Pliny Labs is six engineers in Brooklyn and Lisbon, started by Hugo Tessier (ex-Replit infra) and Yuki Tanaka (ex-Sourcegraph). The full story, including why we named it after a guy who died staring at a volcano, is on the story page.
