About Riverbed

We build the trace you wish your platform already had.

Riverbed is a small, technical team obsessed with one question: when a request goes wrong, can you follow it? Across services, queues, retries, and the parts of your stack no one likes to think about. That answer should be yes — in seconds, not hours.

Mission

Observability that follows the request, not the dashboard.

For fifteen years, observability has been sold as charts. We think that's backwards. The unit of debugging is the request — a single span of work that crosses every boundary your team has drawn around itself. Until you can pull on that thread and watch it unspool through every service, queue, database call and external API, you're guessing.

Our mission is to make that thread the default. Open-source agent at the edge. A hosted query plane that lets you ask the question you actually have. Tracing that survives a deploy on Tuesday afternoon when the on-call engineer has fourteen minutes before standup. No manual instrumentation, no rebuilds, no SDK religion.

Founding story

Riverbed started in late 2022, in a shared office above a bakery in Kreuzberg, with two engineers and a whiteboard full of flame graphs. Mira had spent four years on Stripe's platform team; Toby had been the second SRE at a fintech that grew faster than its tracing budget. Both had watched colleagues spend entire weekends grepping logs for a correlation ID that no one had agreed to propagate.

The first prototype was a single Go binary that sniffed eBPF events and reconstructed spans without a single line of SDK code. It worked. It also fell over the moment we pointed it at a real production workload. The next eighteen months were a long argument with kernel internals, sampling math, and the idea that a query language could be both expressive and fast.

We shipped the agent under Apache 2.0 in March 2024. Six weeks later the first paying team — a logistics platform routing two million events an hour — turned off their previous APM. We took that as a sign.

— Mira & Toby, co-founders

What we believe

Five opinions that shape what we ship.

These aren't posters on a wall. They're the arguments we've already had, written down so the next hire doesn't have to have them again.

  1. The request is the unit of truth.

    Dashboards aggregate the past. Traces explain the present. We design for the engineer who has a customer ticket open in another tab.

  2. Instrumentation is a tax, not a feature.

    If a developer has to touch their code for telemetry to work, we've failed. The agent goes where the request goes, automatically.

  3. Open source is the contract.

    The agent is Apache 2.0 and will stay that way. Your data plane belongs to you. Your spans belong to you. We earn the hosted query plane on its merits.

  4. Pricing should not punish curiosity.

    Per-host, per-seat, per-custom-metric — that math turns engineers into accountants. We charge for retained events, with a free tier that fits real workloads.

  5. Small teams ship sharper products.

    We're 22 people and intend to stay under 60 through the next product surface. Every hire is a decision, not a quota.
