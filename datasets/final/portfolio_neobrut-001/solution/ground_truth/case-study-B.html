01 — The brief

The problem.

Maison Tassel had been pouring perfume in Grasse since 1911 — and selling it through a website that smelled, frankly, of 2014.

Their fourth-generation creative director wanted to launch Nuit d’Octobre, a new oud-and-iris composition, with a digital experience that could carry the weight of the house. The existing site was a static Shopify theme: product grids, lifestyle photography, a footer full of legal text. It converted, but it did not seduce. Press kept describing the brand as “historic” — a polite word, in fragrance, for irrelevant.

The mandate was specific: build a launch site that behaves like a fragrance unfolds — top note, heart, base — and that loads in under 2.5 seconds on a phone in a Parisian Métro tunnel. No autoplay video. No 40MB of textures. And, please, no scroll-jacking that makes the trade press groan.

— Élodie Tassel, Creative Director

“We didn’t want a website. We wanted a vestibule — a place that makes you slow down before you enter the boutique.”

02 — The approach

Three notes,
one site.

We structured the entire experience around the architecture of a fragrance. The homepage opens with the top note: a single bottle, a single line, ambient sound off by default. The product narrative is the heart: scroll-anchored chapters that reveal raw materials, distillation, and the perfumer’s journal. The shop is the base: quiet, dense, with the speed of a checkout that respects intent.

We rebuilt the front-end on Astro with islands of GSAP-driven motion, served textures as AVIF, and used a custom WebGL shader for the iris-petal hero that weighs less than a single Instagram story.

03 — How it shipped

14 weeks,
four phases.

Tassel’s team in Grasse joined a Friday review every week. We kept the scope honest: one launch product, one editorial chapter framework, one shop. Everything else got a Notion ticket marked “phase 2, peut-être.”

Discovery
Weeks 1 – 2

Two days on-site in Grasse. Smelled 41 raw materials. Re-wrote the brand voice from scratch — fewer adjectives, more nouns. Audit of the old stack, a heatmap of the boutique, and a competitive read across 12 niche houses.

Design language
Weeks 3 – 6

Type system around Söhne & a custom italic display. Built the iris shader in three iterations. Defined motion principles: nothing animates without a reason a perfumer would accept.

Build
Weeks 6 – 12

Astro front-end, Sanity for editorial, Shopify Hydrogen for commerce. Weekly Friday demo in Grasse. Performance budget locked at 1.8s LCP — every PR ran a Lighthouse gate.

Launch
Weeks 13 – 14

Soft launch to 400 press & clients with a printed scratch-card invite. Public launch nine days later, coinciding with the Nuit d’Octobre press preview at Hôtel de Crillon.

04 — The outcome

It moved.

Nuit d’Octobre sold out its first run of 1,400 bottles in 11 days. The site became, accidentally, a recruitment tool — Maison Tassel hired a junior perfumer who first wrote in because of the journal.
