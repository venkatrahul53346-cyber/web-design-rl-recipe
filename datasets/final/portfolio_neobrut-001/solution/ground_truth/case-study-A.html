At a glance

A heritage parfumeur, re-pressed for the web.

Founded in Grasse in 1908, Maison Verla had spent a century earning the trust of nez and collector — and almost none of the trust of anyone under forty. They came to us with a question that was really three questions: how do we sell a scent online, how do we keep the ritual, and how do we do both without becoming another minimal-cream-aesthetic perfume brand?

We answered with a site that treats every chapter like a short film and every bottle like a sculpture. The catalog still works. The mystery still works. Both at once.

01 — The Problem
−42%

YoY decline in DTC orders from buyers under 40, 2019–2023.

1 of 6

Chapters of the new collection had any digital presence at launch.

A century of nose. A decade of being invisible online.

Verla’s previous site was a PDF catalog with a buy button bolted on. It told you nothing about why a 110ml flacon of Onde Noire costs €380 — only that it does. The bounce rate was 78%. The story stopped at the front door.

Worse, the house was preparing to release Six Chapters — a serialized collection that demanded narrative pacing the existing site couldn’t hold. We had eleven months until Paris Olympia. We needed a site that could carry a perfumery’s mythology and ship it FedEx the next morning.

02 — The Approach

Treat the catalog like a novel, not a grid.

Three working principles guided every decision — taped to the studio wall in 200pt Inter Tight, naturally.

PRINCIPLE 01

The bottle is the hero. The UI gets out of the way.

We built a real-time WebGL renderer for every flacon — refractive glass, hand-painted labels, the wax seal that catches the light when you tilt your phone. Hover state on desktop spins the bottle; gyroscope on mobile. No video fallbacks; the glass is the same glass everywhere.

→ 11,000 polys / bottle · 2.1MB total bundle · 60fps on iPhone 12

PRINCIPLE 02

Smell with your eyes — synaesthetic typography.

Each of the six chapters has its own type treatment, color regime, and motion signature. Onde Noire drifts vertically like ink in water. Foin Coupé snaps horizontally like a scythe. We composed the system with Verla’s in-house nez, Anaïs Béranger, sketching at the bench. The kerning has a smell.

PRINCIPLE 03

Romance and commerce in the same scroll — no modal in between.

The hardest part wasn’t the WebGL. It was making a checkout that felt like turning the last page of a novel, not abandoning one. We rebuilt Verla’s cart in Hydrogen so it lives in the page — a fixed corner sigil that grows weight as you add. No interruption. No flyout. You leave the chapter when you decide to.

→ Add-to-cart latency: 84ms median · cart abandonment dropped 31%

04 — The Outcome

The first month sold like the first edition of a book.

Verla’s site went live April 11. The Olympia release sold through in 14 days. Three of the six chapters re-pressed within the quarter. Press picked it up before marketing did.
