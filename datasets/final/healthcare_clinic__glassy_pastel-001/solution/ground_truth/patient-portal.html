Privacy & security

Built to protect what you share.

MyCardinal follows HIPAA, HITECH, and the 21st Century Cures Act. Your records are encrypted in transit and at rest, and we never sell or share patient data with advertisers.

How do I enable two-step verification?

After signing in, open Account → Security and choose either text-message codes or an authenticator app. We strongly recommend the authenticator app — it works offline and is more resistant to SIM-swap attacks.

Can I see notes from my doctor's visit?

Yes. Under federal information-blocking rules, clinical notes for most visits are released to your portal as soon as they're signed — usually within a few hours. Pathology and certain sensitive results may carry a brief delay so your physician can call you first.

How do I grant a family member access?

Adults can grant another adult proxy access by completing a short authorisation form (available in the portal or at any reception desk). Parents of children under 12 can self-enrol; for adolescents 12–17, access is limited to comply with state confidentiality law.

Is MyCardinal the right channel for an emergency?

No. The portal is not monitored in real time. For chest pain, stroke symptoms, severe injury, or any life-threatening concern, call 911. For urgent but non-emergency questions outside clinic hours, call our 24-hour nurse line.

If this is a medical emergency, dial 911. For urgent but non-emergency concerns after hours, call our 24-hour nurse line at 1-800-555-0199.
