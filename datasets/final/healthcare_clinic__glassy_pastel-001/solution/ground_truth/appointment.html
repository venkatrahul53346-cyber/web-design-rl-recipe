Frequent questions

Before you submit

A few things patients ask most often when booking their first visit at Cardinal.

Do I need a referral from my primary care doctor?

For most specialties at Cardinal — including cardiology, neurology, and orthopaedics — a referral is helpful but not required. Some insurance plans (HMOs in particular) require a referral for coverage; check with your plan or ask us to verify when we confirm your appointment. Oncology second-opinion visits do not require a referral.

How quickly will I hear back?

Most requests are returned within four business hours. Urgent oncology and cardiac concerns are flagged on intake and routed to a nurse navigator for same-day follow-up. If you submit on a weekend, expect a call by mid-morning Monday.

Can I request a specific doctor?

Yes — note the physician's name in the symptoms field, or use Find a Doctor to review profiles and request directly from their page. We'll do our best to honor preferences while keeping your wait time short.

What if I'm uninsured or between plans?

Cardinal is a non-profit teaching hospital, and financial counselors are available to discuss sliding-scale rates, charity care eligibility, and payment plans before your visit. Choose "Self-pay" on the insurance field and a counselor will reach out alongside scheduling.

Are telehealth visits available?

Yes — most follow-ups and many initial consultations in neurology, medical oncology, and cardiology can be conducted by video through the Patient Portal. Select "Telehealth" as your preferred location and we'll confirm whether your visit type is eligible.

If this is a medical emergency, dial 911. For urgent but non-emergency concerns after hours, call our 24-hour nurse line at 1-800-555-0199.
