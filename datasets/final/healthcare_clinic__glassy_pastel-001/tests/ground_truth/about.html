About Cardinal Medical

Specialty medicine, practised with the patience of a teaching hospital.

For sixty-one years, Cardinal Medical has cared for the people of southern Ohio the way an academic centre should — by pairing every diagnosis with the latest evidence, every patient with a named physician, and every procedure with a team that trains the next generation of clinicians.

Our mission

To advance the health of our region through specialty care that is rigorous, humane, and accessible — and to teach, in everything we do, the doctors and nurses who will carry that work forward.

We are deliberately not the largest hospital system in Ohio. We chose to remain a single-campus academic centre so that cardiology talks to oncology in the hallway, so that an orthopaedic resident can step into a neurosurgical conference, and so that a patient transferred from a community hospital meets the same attending on Tuesday morning that they met on Sunday night. Specialty medicine works better when the people doing it actually know each other.

How we got here

From a 40-bed cardiac ward to a regional academic centre.

Cardinal Medical opened on a wet October morning in 1964 as a forty-bed cardiac ward attached to Greenfield General. The founding physician, Dr. Eleanor Cardinal, had come home from a fellowship at the Cleveland Clinic convinced that rural Ohio deserved the same standard of cardiac care she had practised in the city. She convinced a sceptical hospital board, a more sceptical bank, and — eventually — three young cardiologists to join her. The first patient through the doors was a fifty-eight-year-old farmer named Wendell Pace, who lived another twenty-two years.

The ward became an institute. In 1981 Cardinal Medical affiliated with the Ohio Valley School of Medicine and accepted its first class of internal-medicine residents. Oncology followed in 1989, neurosciences in 1997, and a dedicated orthopaedic and spine programme in 2003. Each expansion was deliberate: we added a specialty only when we could recruit faculty who would teach as well as practise, and only when the volume of patients in our region genuinely needed it.

Today the campus on University Drive includes a 612-bed acute hospital, an outpatient pavilion, a research building shared with the medical school, and the Cardinal Family Cancer Centre, opened in 2019 with the largest single philanthropic gift in our history. Across all of it, the operating principle has not changed: meet the patient where they are, explain what you know and what you don't, and treat the disease with the best evidence available.

What we hold ourselves to

Six commitments that shape every clinical decision.

These aren't slogans. They are the criteria our chiefs use to evaluate departments, the questions our ethics committee asks at every consult, and the standards we measure publicly in our quality reports.

  1. 01

    Evidence before opinion

    Every treatment pathway is reviewed against current literature on a defined cadence. When the evidence changes, our protocols change — even when it's inconvenient.

  2. 02

    Plain language, always

    If a patient or family member cannot repeat back, in their own words, what is happening and why, the conversation isn't finished.

  3. 03

    The named physician

    Every admitted patient has a named attending who is accountable for their care from admission to discharge. Not a service. A person.

  4. 04

    Teaching is patient care

    Residents and fellows learn at the bedside under supervision because that is how good doctors are made — and how attendings stay sharp.

  5. 05

    Access is part of the treatment

    A referral that takes three months is, clinically, a worse referral. We publish our scheduling lead times and work to shorten them.

  6. 06

    Quiet stewardship

    We are a non-profit. Margins return to the work — to recruiting, to research, to charity care — not to growth for its own sake.

Three things we do that most regional hospitals don't

Because we're a teaching centre, the work goes a little deeper.

Train the next physicians

Fourteen ACGME-accredited residency and fellowship programmes, including a competitive cardiology fellowship and a combined neurology/neurosurgery track.

Run our own trials

312 active protocols across our four institutes — from phase II oncology trials to device studies in structural heart, available to patients without leaving the region.

Publish what we learn

Quality outcomes, infection rates, and surgical volumes are reported publicly each quarter. We believe a teaching hospital should show its work.
