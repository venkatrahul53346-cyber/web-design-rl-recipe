Public services of the National Archive

Eleven services are available to the public. Catalogue search and the open datasets portal require no account. Records requests, reading-room access, and reproduction orders require a free researcher account and, in some cases, identity verification.

01 — DISCOVERY

Catalogue & search

Search descriptions of every record we hold, from cabinet papers of 1937 to last quarter's census tables. Roughly 27% of catalogue entries link to a digitised file you can read in the browser.

The catalogue uses full-text search across descriptions, dates, departments, and persons named. You can narrow by holding (e.g. Cabinet Papers, Colonial Office, Vital Statistics), by access status (open, partly closed, under review), and by format. Saved searches require a free account.

02 — DISCLOSURE

Records requests (Freedom of Information)

Request access to records that are not yet open to the public, including departmental correspondence, internal reviews, and case files still inside the 30-year rule.

The Archive accepts requests on behalf of all federal bodies under section 14 of the Records Act. Requests are routed to the holding department; the Archive acts as the front door and tracks compliance. You will be notified within five working days whether your request has been accepted, transferred, or refused, and a substantive response is due within twenty working days.

03 — ON-SITE

Reading room access

Original records and fragile materials that cannot be digitised may be consulted in person at one of two reading rooms. Booking is required and seats are released fourteen days in advance.

When you book a seat, list the catalogue references you intend to consult. Staff will retrieve the items from secure storage; large or sensitive items may take up to two working days. Pencils, laptops, and phone cameras are permitted; pens, ink, and bags are not. The Capitol room seats 84 readers and is open Tuesday–Saturday; Northgate seats 42 and is open by appointment for restricted holdings.

04 — COPIES

Reproductions & certified copies

Order high-resolution scans, certified paper copies, or microfilm prints of items in our holdings. Certified copies bear the Archivist's seal and are admissible in court.

Standard scans (600 dpi, TIFF + JPEG) are 4.50 € per page. Bound volumes and items larger than A2 are quoted individually. For commercial reproduction in print or broadcast, a separate publication licence applies; consult the licensing office before placing an order.

05 — OPEN DATA

Open datasets & API

428 structured datasets — from the 1881 census to monthly parliamentary voting records — are published under the Open Government Licence and consumable via REST and bulk download.

The API is rate-limited to 1,000 requests per hour for anonymous use and 20,000 per hour with a free key. All endpoints return JSON; bulk dumps are also available as CSV, Parquet, and SQLite. Schemas are versioned and breaking changes are announced ninety days in advance through the changelog.

06 — TRANSFER

Transfer of records from agencies

Federal departments and statutory bodies transfer records of permanent value to the Archive on a rolling schedule. This service is not for members of the public.

Records keepers should consult their disposal authority before initiating a transfer. The Archive accepts paper, microform, and digital records that meet our preservation specifications (PDF/A-2, TIFF, FFV1/MKV for audiovisual). A transfer manifest must accompany every shipment, and physical transfers are scheduled fortnightly.
