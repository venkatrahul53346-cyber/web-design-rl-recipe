Departments &
operational divisions

The National Archive is organised into nine departments responsible for accessioning, preservation, public access, and the administration of statutory records. Use this directory to identify the custodian of a series, request a finding aid, or contact a department lead directly.

DEPT-01

Accessioning & Appraisal

Receives transfers from government bodies, appraises records of permanent value, and issues disposal schedules under the Public Records Act §14.

Accepting transfers
Head · M. Adelekan Staff · 48 Floor · B2 / East wing
DEPT-02

Preservation & Conservation

Maintains the physical integrity of paper, parchment, photographic, and audiovisual holdings. Operates the conservation studio and environmental monitoring programme.

Studio open 3-month queue
Head · R. Ostrowska Staff · 34 Floor · B1 / Studio 4
DEPT-03

Digitisation Programme

Captures, processes, and publishes digital surrogates of high-demand series. Operates eight production scanning lines and the public image-order workflow.

All lines operational
Head · K. Vasquez Staff · 62 Floor · L1 / Bay D
DEPT-04

Public Access & Reading Rooms

Manages on-site access to original records, issues reader's tickets, supervises the four reading rooms, and coordinates supervised handling of restricted materials.

Tue–Sat 09:00–17:00
Head · S. Pereira Staff · 71 Floor · L2 / Reading Room 1
DEPT-05

Cataloguing & Description

Produces and maintains finding aids to ISAD(G) standard. Responsible for the public catalogue, authority records, and the Cartographic & Architectural Records collection.

In progress Backlog 11.4%
Head · D. Fischer Staff · 53 Floor · L3 / North
DEPT-06

Freedom of Information

Processes statutory access requests under the Freedom of Information Act 2000 and reviews records held under closure for early release.

Avg. response 22 days
Head · A. Nwosu Staff · 29 Floor · L4 / West
DEPT-07

Government Records Liaison

Advises ministries and agencies on records management, retention schedules, and the secure transfer of permanent records to the Archive.

Advisory open
Head · T. Bekele Staff · 22 Floor · L4 / East
DEPT-08

Heritage & Outreach

Curates public exhibitions in the South Gallery, coordinates schools programmes, manages press enquiries, and delivers the annual Open Archives week.

Exhibition: Lines on the Map
Head · I. Marchetti Staff · 18 Floor · L1 / South Gallery
DEPT-09

Digital Preservation Lab

Ingests born-digital records, maintains format migration pathways, and operates the dark-archive replication infrastructure across three geographic sites.

Ingest active Public tours suspended
Head · J. Hara Staff · 40 Floor · B3 / Secure

Governance

The Archive is a non-ministerial department reporting to the Minister for the Cabinet Office. The Director is appointed by the Crown for a single seven-year term under the Public Records Act 1958 (as amended 2011).

Working with a department

For researchers

Identify the custodian of a series

Most series are held by a single department for their full lifecycle. Use the catalogue to find the series reference, then contact the relevant head via the directory above.

Open catalogue
For government bodies

Transfer records to the Archive

Government Records Liaison (DEPT-07) advises on retention schedules, the 20-year rule, and the technical specifications for paper and digital transfers.

Begin transfer process
For journalists

Submit a press enquiry

All media enquiries are handled by Heritage & Outreach (DEPT-08). Expedited responses are available for time-sensitive stories with documented deadlines.
