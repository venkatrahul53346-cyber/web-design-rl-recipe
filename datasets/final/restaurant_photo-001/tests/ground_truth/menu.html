The menu · Late autumn

Nine courses, written the morning of.

What follows is the menu we are serving this week. It will change by Thursday next — perhaps a fish, perhaps the squash that finally turned. We cook what the field gives us, and the field is not in a hurry.

i. — to begin

Snacks from the garden

Brought to the table together, eaten with your hands, while the kitchen finishes the bread.

Buckwheat tart, smoked trout roe
trout from McFadden Farm, three miles south
Field

A thin shell of toasted buckwheat, crème fraîche whipped with horseradish from the back row, and a spoon of orange roe cured in our own salt.

Roasted koshihikari rice, nori, brown butter
rice from Massa Organics, Sacramento Valley
Vegetarian

One perfect mouthful of crisped rice, dusted with seaweed we toast each morning, finished with butter cooked dark in a copper pan.

Chicory leaf, anchovy, lardo
chicories grown in row 14

A single bitter leaf, draped with cured pork fat from the Berkshire we raised last spring, and a half anchovy from the Cantabrian.

ii. — from the garden

First courses

Cool, raw, and very close to the ground. We pull these things in the hour before service.

Sungold tomato, last of the season
picked Thursday morning, hoop house three
VeganField

Halved and dressed only with green olive oil from a friend in Hopland, a few grains of finishing salt, and basil leaves the size of your thumbnail.

Hokkaido squash, hazelnut, brown bread
squash cured six weeks in the barn
Vegetarian

Roasted whole until collapsing, served warm with toasted hazelnuts, crumbs of rye bread, and a spoonful of yogurt from Stornetta dairy.

Hand-cut tagliatelle, walnut, sage brown butter
eggs from our forty-three hens

Pasta rolled to order from flour milled Wednesday in Petaluma, sauced quietly with black walnuts we cracked in October and sage from the herb bed.

iii. — from the coast

The fish course

Whatever Albion landed that morning, cooked simply, with what the garden offered alongside it.

Black cod, fennel pollen, brassica greens
line-caught off Albion, Wednesday evening

Cured in salt for an hour, then cooked skin-down in a single cast pan until the edges crisp. Served on a bed of mustard greens wilted in cod fat.

iv. — from the field

Mains

A single shared plate, brought to the center of the table. We carve at the pass and serve from a wooden board.

Lamb shoulder, fire-roasted & rested overnight
Stemple Creek Ranch, Marin coast
Shared

Rubbed with crushed coriander and rosemary, cooked over oak embers for nine hours, then rested the full night before being warmed and pulled to the bone.

Heirloom beans & bitter greens
Rancho Gordo, Napa
Vegan

Yellow eye beans simmered with onion and bay, finished with the last of our escarole, a great deal of black pepper, and a slick of olive oil.

Hearth bread, cultured butter, farm salt
baked in the wood oven at three p.m.

Naturally leavened over four days, baked dark, served warm. Butter churned Wednesday from Stornetta cream. The salt is ours, evaporated in a flat pan on the porch.

v. — to end

Dessert & after

Two small things, a coffee if you like, and a glass of something amber on the porch.

Quince poached in late-harvest riesling
quince from the old tree by the road
Vegetarian

Cooked slowly until the color of garnet, served warm with a quenelle of crème épaisse and a brittle of toasted oats.

Buckwheat honey ice cream, sea salt
honey from Bee Hunter Wines & Apiary

Made in the morning, scooped at the table, finished with a turn of black pepper and a few crystals of our coarse finishing salt.

Petits fours — three small bites
whatever was beautiful that afternoon

A walnut shortbread, a square of olive oil cake with fig jam, and a single dark chocolate filled with bay leaf cream.

A few notes

The menu is set. We are happy to accommodate allergies and intolerances with at least forty-eight hours’ notice — please write to us when you reserve. We cannot offer substitutions on the night.

We are not able to serve a vegetarian or vegan version of the full menu in the dining room, but we do offer a dedicated plant-based tasting on Sundays with notice. Children twelve and older are welcome.

Service runs roughly two and a half hours. The kitchen closes after the eight o’clock seating; if you’d like to linger on the porch with a glass of something, we’d love that.
