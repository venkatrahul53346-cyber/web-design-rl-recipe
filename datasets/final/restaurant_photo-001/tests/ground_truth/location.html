Getting here

The drive is part of the meal. Plan it like one.

From San Francisco

132 miles · 2 hr 35 min

North on Highway 101 to Cloverdale, then west on Highway 128 through the redwoods. The road narrows past Boonville — drive it in daylight if you can. Coming home, fog often closes in along the Navarro by 10pm.

From Mendocino Coast

28 miles · 45 min

Inland on Highway 128 from the village of Albion. You will follow the river upstream for most of the drive. The orchards begin at Navarro; we are seven miles further on, past Greenwood Ridge Vineyards.

From Healdsburg

68 miles · 1 hr 25 min

The scenic route — north on 101, then a hard left at Cloverdale onto 128. A good driver can make it in an hour and ten, but the road is full of switchbacks. Most guests pause for a glass at Pennyroyal in Boonville.

Before you arrive

A few practical notes —

Reading these will save you a phone call from the gate.

  • Parking Complimentary, gravel lot at the bay laurels. Twenty-two spaces. The lot is unlit; we provide a lantern walk to the dining room. Please do not park along Greenwood Ridge Road — CalFire keeps the shoulders clear.
  • Cell service None on property. Verizon flickers at the front gate; AT&T returns about a mile down the ridge. The restaurant has a landline at (707) 895-4400 if you need to be reached.
  • Arrival window Doors open thirty minutes before your seating. Come early — there is a garden walk, a glass of something cold, and a fire in the courtyard most evenings.
  • Public transit There is none, honestly. The nearest Greyhound stop is Ukiah, fifty miles east. We can arrange a car service from Healdsburg or the Sonoma County airport with two days' notice — ask when you book.
  • Accessibility The dining room and restrooms are step-free. The path from the lot is packed gravel for about sixty feet; we are glad to bring a chair or arm to anyone who would prefer one. Let us know in advance.
  • What to wear Comfortable, with a layer. The valley drops fifteen degrees after sundown and a portion of service moves through the garden. No dress code — people show up in everything from jeans to silk.
  • Staying nearby We keep a short list of inns we trust. The Boonville Hotel (eight miles), Brambles Bed & Breakfast (three miles), and the cottages at Goldeneye Winery. Email hello@saltandfield.com and we will send the current list.

Lost on the ridge? Call the kitchen.
