The room

“The pantry is a forty-acre walk. We cook what is ready, and not much else.”

Marisol Vega · Chef & Co-owner

Dinner at Salt & Field is a single nightly menu of nine to eleven courses, written the morning of service. The dining room sits inside a restored 1908 hay barn at the eastern edge of the farm, with the kitchen open along one wall and the herb garden five steps from the pass.

The farm

Forty acres,
one menu.

We grow eighty percent of what reaches the plate. The rest comes from neighbors we can call by name — the Holsens for lamb, Sea Star Sea Farm in Mendocino for oysters, Aimee at Filigreen for stone fruit and apples.

Ana Reyes runs the field. She farms without synthetic inputs, rotates beds on a seven-year cycle, and keeps a pair of Tamworth pigs that eat the kitchen’s trim and return it to the soil. The menu is written downstream of her week.

Growing practices

Field

Vegetables, herbs, flowers

Forty rotating beds along the south slope. Ana’s notebook is the closest thing we have to a menu plan — what’s tender, what’s peaking, what needs eating this week.

Orchard

Stone fruit, apples, quince

Eighty-six trees, most planted by the previous owners in the 1970s. Gravensteins, Bartletts, a single old medlar we use once a year for a single course in November.

Pasture

Hens, sheep, two pigs

A small flock of Buff Orpingtons supplies eggs for the cracker course. The lambs live three pastures up and are processed on-farm by Marin Sun Farms.
