The House

Dinner is what grew this week, cooked over fire and cared for very closely.

Salt & Field sits at the end of a gravel road in the Wallowa Valley, where eighty acres of pasture, orchard and market garden feed a forty-seat dining room three nights a week. The menu is decided each Thursday morning, after a walk through the rows with the kitchen team, and printed in the afternoon. There are no choices on the card and no dishes that travel far to reach it.

Seven guest rooms above the granary make it possible to stay through breakfast — a slow, quiet meal of eggs from the coop, sourdough from the wood oven, and whatever fruit the trees are giving up. Most guests drive in from Portland, Boise or Walla Walla. A few hike down from the Eagle Cap Wilderness with mud on their boots. We welcome both.

Seven Rooms

Stay above the granary.

Seven rooms with cast-iron tubs, wool blankets, and views of the orchard or the river. Breakfast is included; dinner is a short walk across the yard. From $295 a night, two-night minimum on weekends.

On the Farm

Walks, harvests & the honey house.

Saturday mornings, our farm manager Cal leads a two-hour walk through the gardens, pastures and orchards — finishing with coffee and a slice of something warm in the honey house. Free for house guests, $30 for the public.
