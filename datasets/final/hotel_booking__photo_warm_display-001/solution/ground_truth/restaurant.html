A note from the kitchen

From field to plate, in roughly four hundred feet.

The walk from the kitchen door to the lettuces is shorter than the walk from your table to the restroom. That proximity isn't a marketing line — it's the entire reason the menu exists in the form it does. Greens are pulled at four, dressed at six, on the plate by quarter past seven.

Chef Marin Aldaco trained under Magnus Nilsson at Fäviken and spent four years at Blue Hill at Stone Barns before moving the family to Wallowa County in 2021. The kitchen she's built here is small — six cooks including her — and quiet by design. We don't shout orders. The pass faces the dining room.

Most of what arrives at your table was alive on this farm within the last seventy-two hours. The pork is from our Berkshire-Tamworth cross, raised on whey from the cheese kitchen. The honey is from the hives behind the barn. The salt is from the Pacific, two hundred and ninety miles west, brought in once a season by a friend who drives a truck.

Everything else — the wine, the bread flour, the coffee — comes from people we know by name. Read more about our growers and producers.

The shape of an evening

A few things to know before you come.

Arrival

Six-thirty in the orchard

Guests arrive at 6:30 PM for a glass of something cold under the apple trees, weather permitting. The dining room opens at seven.

Dress as you'd like

No code. Most guests come in from a hike or a long drive. Layers are kind — evenings cool quickly here.

Children

We welcome children twelve and older. The kitchen happily prepares a thoughtful version of the menu for them.

Dietary

Written in parallel

A full vegetarian menu is written every week alongside the omnivore one — not as a substitution, but as its own thing.

Allergies & restrictions

Tell us when you book. We accommodate gluten-free, dairy-free, and most allergies with at least 48 hours' notice.

Pescatarian & vegan

Both possible with notice. Vegan menus draw heavily on the cellar of ferments and preserves we build through the summer.

The room

Forty seats, one room

The dining room was a hay barn until 2022. Cedar beams, a long open hearth, candles only after sundown.

The pass

The kitchen is open to the dining room. Marin and the cooks are happy to talk between courses — wave them down.

Private dining

The full room may be booked for parties of twenty-eight to forty. Write to us for terms.

Booking

Six weeks ahead

We release reservations in calendar-month blocks, six weeks before service. The list is small — book early.

The deposit

A $90/guest deposit is taken at booking and applied to your final bill. Refundable up to seven days before.

The waitlist

If your night is full, join the list — we release seats most weeks as plans change. Sundays open up most often.
