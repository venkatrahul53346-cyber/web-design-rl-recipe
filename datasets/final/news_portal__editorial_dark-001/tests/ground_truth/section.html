A working glossary · terms we use a lot

Topics A–Z →
Fond
The browned residue stuck to the bottom of a pan after searing. The most underused free flavour in any kitchen. Dissolve it with wine, stock, or water before it burns.
Bulk ferment
The first long rise of a dough, before shaping. Where flavour and structure are built. Time it by appearance, not the clock.
Temper
To bring an ingredient — eggs, chocolate, gelatine — gently to a target temperature so it behaves predictably afterward. Less alchemy than housekeeping.
Mise en place
Everything ready before you start. The single most boring habit that will improve your cooking the most.
Brunoise
A two-millimetre dice. Worth learning once; useful for the rest of your life, particularly with carrots and celeriac.
Reduction
Boiling a liquid down to concentrate it. Patience, mostly. Salt last, taste twice.
