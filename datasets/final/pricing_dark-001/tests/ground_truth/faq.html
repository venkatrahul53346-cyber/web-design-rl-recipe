Getting started01 / 04
How long does it actually take to see a trace?

If you're on a runtime we support out of the box — Node, Go, Python, Java, Ruby, .NET — the median is about seven minutes from npm install to seeing your first end-to-end trace in the query plane. Drop the agent in, set RIVERBED_KEY, redeploy one service, and traffic shows up. You don't need to instrument the other services to see the calls between them; the agent infers spans from outbound HTTP, gRPC, and database protocols.

The longest part is usually waiting for your CI to redeploy.

Do I need to rip out OpenTelemetry to use Riverbed?

No. The Riverbed agent speaks OTLP natively. If you're already exporting OTel spans, point the exporter at ingest.riverbed.io:4317 and the spans land in the same query plane as the agent's auto-captured ones. Most teams run both side by side for the first month and then decide whether the manual instrumentation is still pulling its weight.

Will the agent slow down my services?

The hot path adds roughly 40–120 μs per span depending on language and payload. Span emission is non-blocking and batched off a ring buffer; if the local collector falls behind, the agent drops samples rather than back-pressuring your request. We publish a continuous benchmark against a 1M rps reference workload — the overhead chart is in the docs under Performance characteristics.

What languages and runtimes are supported?

First-class auto-instrumentation: Node 18+, Go 1.20+, Python 3.9+, Java 11+, Ruby 3.0+, and .NET 6+. For Rust, Elixir, and PHP we ship thin OTel-compatible SDKs — auto-capture is limited there but the rest of the query plane works identically.

Pricing & billing02 / 04
What does the free tier actually include?

50 million spans per month, 7 days of retention, three seats, and unlimited services. No credit card. No "trial expires in 14 days" email. Teams have run small production workloads on the free tier for over a year — we'd rather you graduate to Pro because you need 30-day retention than because we throttled you.

How is a "span" counted? I want to do the math before I commit.

One span equals one operation: an HTTP handler invocation, a database query, a queue publish, a downstream RPC. A typical request graph in a microservices app produces 8–25 spans. We do not bill for dropped spans, sampled-out spans, or spans rejected by ingestion. Your monthly usage report breaks the count down by service so you can find the noisy ones.

What happens if I exceed my plan's span quota mid-month?

Two options, set per workspace. Soft cap (default): we keep ingesting and bill overage at $0.20 per million spans, prorated. Hard cap: we begin tail-sampling once you hit 100% and surface a banner in the UI. No surprise invoices and no service interruption either way.

Can I switch plans or cancel without talking to anyone?

Yes. Upgrades take effect immediately; downgrades take effect on your next billing cycle. Cancellation is a button in Settings → Billing — no "book a call to discuss your needs." Your data stays queryable for 30 days after cancellation so you can export it.

Agent & data03 / 04
Is the agent really open source?

Yes — Apache 2.0, on GitHub, all of it: the collector, the auto-instrumentation shims, the wire protocol, and the local UI for offline debugging. The hosted query plane is the commercial product. You can self-host the agent forever and never send a span our way; we won't notice and you won't owe us anything.

How does Riverbed handle sensitive request data?

The agent runs a redaction pass before anything leaves your host. By default it strips known PII patterns (emails, credit-card numbers, JWTs in headers) and any field matching configurable regex rules. You can also pin specific span attributes to local-only — they'll appear in the local UI but never cross the network boundary.

Where is my trace data stored?

By default, in our US-East region (Virginia). EU customers can pin to Frankfurt at provisioning time. Enterprise plans support BYO bucket — your spans land in your S3 or GCS bucket and we query against it. We never replicate trace bodies across regions without an explicit configuration change.

Security & ops04 / 04
What's your compliance posture?

SOC 2 Type II (annual), ISO 27001, and HIPAA BAA available on Enterprise. We are not FedRAMP authorized today; there's a draft on the roadmap for Q4. The security portal at trust.riverbed.io hosts the reports, sub-processor list, and pen-test summaries — no NDA required for the index.

Do you offer an SLA, and what's the credit math?

Pro: 99.9% on ingestion and query. Enterprise: 99.95% with regional failover. Credits accrue automatically against the affected billing cycle — 10% credit at first breach, 25% below 99.5%, 50% below 99.0%. You don't have to file a ticket; the credits show up on the next invoice with the incident IDs attached.
