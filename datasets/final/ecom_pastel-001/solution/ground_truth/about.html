The Workshop — Napoli

Four pieces, made slowly.

We share a small building on Vico Lungo Gelso with a printer, a violin restorer, and the family who taught us to knit. We make four styles a year. That is all we have ever wanted to make.

Our intent

Wren & Bell exists to keep a particular kind of knitwear alive — the kind made on slow Italian machines by people whose hands remember a pattern before the screen does.

We design garments to be worn for a decade, not a season. A cardigan should soften with washing. A crewneck should hold its shoulder line after a hundred wears. The merino should pill once, gently, and then stop. We test for those things because we wear what we make.

Nothing here is on sale, ever. The price you see is what the piece costs to make well, plus what it costs to run a small house. We will tell you the breakdown if you ask.

How we began

A workshop, a winter, and a borrowed machine.

In the winter of 2019, Elena moved home to Naples to clear out her grandmother's apartment. In the back room she found a Shima Seiki SES-122 — bought in 1986, used to make sweaters for a department store that no longer exists. It still worked. The pattern cards were stacked in a shoebox under the bed.

Tomás had been writing about garment industry waste for a magazine in London and was, frankly, tired of writing. He came to help for two weeks. He stayed. We knit our first cardigan in February 2020 on yarn from a mill in Biella that had four kilos to spare.

We did not have a plan beyond making something we would want to wear forever. We still don't, really. We make four pieces a year because that is what eleven pairs of hands can do without rushing.

Elena Bellini & Tomás Wren — Founders, Napoli
House principles

Six things we will not negotiate.

Some of these cost us money. A few cost us speed. None of them are marketing — they are simply how the workshop runs.

01

Yarn before pattern.

We design around what the mills have spun well that year. If the cashmere isn't right, the cardigan waits. We have skipped two drops in five years for this reason.

02

Fully-fashioned, fully linked.

Every shoulder, neck and side seam is linked by hand rather than overlocked. It takes forty minutes per crewneck and lasts decades longer.

03

Repair, always.

If a Wren & Bell piece develops a hole in the first ten years, we fix it. Send it back; we'll cover postage one way. A small note arrives with the mended garment.

04

One price, plainly.

No sales, no friends-and-family, no Black Friday. The price reflects the work. Discounting it would be unkind to the people who paid in full last week.

05

Small drops, on purpose.

240 pieces per style means we can know each one. It also means we don't burn yarn we can't sell. Scarcity is a consequence of the size of the room, not a strategy.

06

Quiet, by default.

No influencer seeding, no paid placements, no urgency banners. If a piece is worth wearing, it will find the person who needs it.

Materials & care

Two fibres, three mills, no blends we can't trace.

Our cashmere comes from Cariaggi in Cagli; our merino from Zegna Baruffa in Biella. Neither mill will sell to us if we ask for more than they can make well. We wash every garment once before it ships — cold water, no softener, flat-dried on a wooden frame.
