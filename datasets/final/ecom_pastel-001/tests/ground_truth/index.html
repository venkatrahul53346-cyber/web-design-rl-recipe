No. 01 — The Cammino Crewneck

A crewneck that took us nine months and three trips to Biella.

We rejected six yarns before settling on a 4-ply Mongolian cashmere spun in Piedmont — heavier than the industry standard, finished with steam rather than chemical washing.

It softens with wear. It does not pill in the first month, because we do not cheat the gauge.

Read the construction notes →
01 / Place

Knit in Naples, by hand-fed machine.

Our workshop sits above a courtyard in the Sanità. Six Stoll machines, two finishers, one linker. No middlemen, no white-label factory.

02 / Pace

Four releases a year. No restocks.

When a colour sells out, it is out. We would rather close a style than rush a re-run on the wrong yarn lot.

03 / Repair

Mended, free, for five years.

Send us a hole or a snagged sleeve and we will fix it on the original linker — invisibly, and without charging you for it.

A note from the workshop

We started Wren & Bell because the knitwear we wanted no longer existed at a price a person could justify. Not the cashmere of the eighties — the kind that holds its shape after a decade — but the version of it being sold today, thinned and rushed and finished by a machine that doesn't know the yarn.

So we found Luigi, who has run the same six knitting frames for thirty-eight years, and we agreed to make four pieces. Then four more. Then four more.

If you only ever buy one of our sweaters, we will have done our job.

— Greta & Tomas, founders
