Description −

The Gattuso is the cardigan we couldn't find anywhere else: a 12-gauge knit with the weight of an overcoat but the give of a sweater. The body is worked in one panel; the shoulders are hand-linked. Five corozo buttons from a small turner outside Cremona. We make it four times a year — never more.

Materials & provenance +
Yarn
100% Mongolian cashmere, two-ply, undyed.
Spinner
Filatura Cariaggi, Cagli (IT).
Knit
Wren & Bell workshop, Napoli — Stoll CMS machines, 1986.
Buttons
Corozo nut, Bottoneria Lombarda.
Measurements +

Size M: chest 56 cm, length 68 cm, sleeve 64 cm. Model is 1.82 m and wears M. Sits loose at the body, close at the cuff. We recommend taking your usual size; the knit blocks softly with the first wash.

Care & repair +

Hand wash cold with a wool soap, or dry clean. Dry flat. We mend any Wren & Bell piece — moth holes, snagged seams, a worn elbow — for as long as we are knitting. Send it to Napoli; we pay return postage.

Shipping & returns +

Complimentary in the EU and UK; €18 elsewhere. Thirty days to return, unworn, in the canvas pouch we send it in.

How this piece is made

Six hours on a Stoll from 1986.

Our machines were built in the year Maradona won the league. They knit slower than anything modern, but they hold tension the way old machines do — evenly, quietly, for decades.

Each Gattuso passes through four hands before it leaves Vico Lungo Gelso.

  1. KnitOne panel at a time, 12-gauge, full-fashioned at the armhole.
  2. LinkShoulders and neckband joined stitch-for-stitch by hand.
  3. WashCold soak in soft water to bloom the cashmere.
  4. PressSteamed flat on a wool blanket; never tumbled.
